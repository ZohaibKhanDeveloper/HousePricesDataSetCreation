At the east of River Chenab lies Multan, the City of Saints. The city holds unique cultural and spiritual significance in Pakistan; it is known as Madina-tul-Auliya because of the large number of shrines there.

Historically, it was also known as Kashtpur, Hanspur, and Sanabpur. Its current name was derived from the city’s ancient temple, Mulasthan. 

Being an industrial and commercial city, Multan is connected to Lahore and Karachi via railway and road. It is one of the fastest-growing metropolia of Pakistan and, hence, there are many promising investment opportunities in this city. With the launch of housing projects like DHA Multan, Royal Grace Housing Society, and other projects on Bosan Road and Khanewal Road, the real estate sector has a great margin for growth here. 

Commercial projects like Sharif Mall and Fashion Mall are also attracting a lot of attention, by offering an amazing mix of shopping outlets, restaurants, offices, and modern residential apartments to the residents.

Other developmental projects have also contributed to the growth of the real estate sector. For instance, the Multan Metro Bus project completed in early 2017 provided a large-scale rapid transit system to the residents. This, in turn, gradually increased the value of many housing schemes along its route. 

Facilities in Multan

Being one of the most important dry ports of Pakistan, Multan is connected to many major cities like Bahawalpur, Muzaffargarh, Rahimyar Khan, Dera Ghazi Khan, and Faisalabad. Moreover, some of the most prestigious universities like the Bahauddin Zakariya University, Medical and Dental College, and Nishtar Medical College are also located here.

Modern housing and commercial projects like WAPDA Town, Garden Town, Model Town, Shalimar Road Colony, Shah Town Kumharanwala, Bahadur Put Cantt, and Zakariya Town are offering some of the most comfortable and luxurious residential projects in Multan. All of these housing schemes are equipped with state-of-the-art facilities like hospitals, parks, schools and colleges, restaurants, and mosques.

Leisure Activities in Multan

The most popular tourist spots in Multan include:

  • Yardage Shuhada Park
  • Fort Kohna
  • Bahauddin Zakriya Tomb
  • Shrine of Shah Yousaf Gardezi
  • CSD Park
  • Ghanta Ghar
  • Delhi Gate
  • Noor Mehal
  • Laal Sahara
  • Museum of Bibi Pak Daman

Security in Multan

Multan has witnessed one of the lowest crime rates in Pakistan in the past few years. Moreover, there are several CCTV cameras installed throughout the city to improve the surveillance system. As a result, the law and order situation of the city makes it a safe place to invest in.

Prices of Houses for Sale in Multan

The sizes of houses for sale in Multan include 5 marla, 10 marla, 1 kanal, 2 kanal, and 4 kanal. There are also villas in Multan Cantt that have been designed by top architects from all over the country. High-quality materials have been used in their construction, and their designs are in accordance with contemporary trends in the market.

The price trends of houses for sale in Multan depend on various factors, which include the location, housing unit, amenities being offered etc. Based on these, the price range for 5 marla, 10 marla, and 1 kanal plots are as follows:

  • To buy a 5 marla house in Multan, you need a budget between Rs. 38 lac and Rs. 75 lac.
  • If you want to buy an 8 marla house in Multan, the prices will range from Rs. 60 lac to Rs. 2.35 crore.
  • If you are planning to buy a spacious house of 1 kanal, the budget should be between Rs. 1.25 crore and Rs. 4.25 crore.
